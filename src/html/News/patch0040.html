Update

Patch 0.0.40

The CBT continues...

We’re back with another patch for the Calamari Alpha Playtest, and this time, we’re making some pretty heavy gameplay adjustments based on the last round of feedback. Armor has been completely reworked, DBNO is getting a serious overhaul, and frag grenades? Yeah, those were a bit much.

Let’s go ahead and break it all down.

-Armor Value Changes

We’ve shifted from using a character’s defense value for rolls to using their armor value instead. This should create a more balanced combat experience, where every shot has a chance to hit, but cover plays a key role in mitigating damage. Speaking of which, cover is also getting a slight tweak:

  • Light cover provides a +1 to the defense roll.
  • Heavy cover provides a +2.

Additionally, armor values are now classified under an 'Armor Class' (AC) system for clarity:

  • AC1: Standard protection—plate carrier, helmet, and similar gear.
  • AC2: Sapper-level protection—PC + Ballistic Visor, groin flap, and extended coverage. (Most Exo-Yakuza will be here.)
  • AC3: Juggernaut-tier—EOD suits and other full-body armor. (Boss-tier Yakuza fall here.)

Vehicle armor remains a separate mechanic for now.

-DBNO Changes

Previously, 'Down But Not Out' was an alternative to instant death on lower difficulties. Now, we’ve refined it to represent crippling injuries—hits that take you out of the fight but don’t immediately kill you. Think shoulder wounds, gut shots, or knee injuries that require immediate medical attention.

-Frag Nerf

Yeah, frags were completely busted. With their original 12 damage, they trivialized fights—tossing one into a room and rolling a minimal MOS was practically a guaranteed kill.

We’re dialing that back:

  • Frags now deal 8 damage.
  • They require an MOS of 2 for an instant kill on enemies.
  • They now push a level 1 player into DBNO instead of outright removing them.

-Wound Calculations

We realized some wound mechanics weren’t really working as intended, particularly around cover and simple actions. The goal here is to make sure wounds matter without punishing players for taking cover to recover.

Here’s what’s changing:

  • Wounds no longer affect cover rolls.
  • Wounds no longer affect simple actions like taking painkillers or using injectors.
  • Wounds still affect all other action rolls.

-Movement Buff

We’re adding a more defined movement system with four tiers: Crawling, Walking, Running, and Sprinting.

  • Running allows you to take a standard action with a penalty while moving.
  • Sprinting means you’re in a full-on dash to cover—no actions, but you arrive in the same turn instead of the next.

-Final Notes

These changes will be live in the CBT branch today for those with access. If you’re still playing through the mini-campaign in regular RP6, don’t worry—you’re not missing out on anything just yet.

As always, let us know what you think in the feedback channels. And oh yeah—Merry Christmas, gang.

- Spiny

Patch Notes 0.0.40

Bug Fixes:

  • Fixed a bug where armor values were not properly factoring into defense rolls.
  • Fixed an issue where certain movement animations were playing incorrectly.
  • Fixed a bug where frag grenades ignored MOS calculations.
  • Fixed a glitch where DBNO status effects were incorrectly stacking.

Gameplay Changes:

  • Switched defensive rolls from defense value to armor value.
  • Reclassified armor values into AC1, AC2, and AC3 tiers.
  • Reworked DBNO to function as a crippling injury system.
  • Adjusted frag grenade damage from 12 to 8.
  • Updated MOS requirements for frag grenade instant kills.
  • Wound penalties no longer apply to cover rolls or simple actions.
  • Implemented new movement mechanics, adding Running and Sprinting states.

Improvements and Additions:

  • Improved movement fluidity and responsiveness.
  • Enhanced cover mechanics to work with new movement rules.
  • Adjusted DBNO animations for better visual clarity.
  • Rebalanced enemy armor to reflect new AC classifications.
  • General performance optimizations and stability improvements.
