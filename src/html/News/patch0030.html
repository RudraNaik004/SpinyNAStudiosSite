Update

Patch 0.03

Snake eyes... Again.

Man I am so happy we never made that May release window for Calamari since good lord our QA testing was not as rigorous as what our playtesters are putting the game through. But I digress, and am extremely happy either way.

Welcome to Patch 0.03 of the Calamari Alpha Playtest. As a lot of you know by now we’ve shifted away from the old director system, scrapped any remnants of the Genesis system, and moved into integrating SCUMM for a more dynamic gameplay experience. Though, as we’ve learned from the first playtest a few weeks back—a lot of things need tweaking. So let’s go through some of the dev notes for the 0.03 patch, shall we?

-Improved check overlay

One thing we noticed is that during dialogue, when the 'director' threw out a check to provide information to players, was that the interface was prone to look a little confusing at times, and also be a bit slow to show information. We want to distinguish information from a check with actual prose and dialogue a bit to reduce confusion before it becomes something we need to worry about later. So we’re going to push a change this patch that might look a bit familiar...

Computers [Success] - You understand the term 'director' from an earlier patch notes. It was mentioned that it was the main way that the game determines what encounters happen, and how they happen. Almost like a movie director and exactly like how a specific valve game implemented it, one that was left for dead.


Trivia [Failure] - The presentation of this information vexes you. The name is on the tip of your tongue and it sounds like some sort of dance game… At least you think it does. It did have ‘disco’ in it.

-Armor and Cover

Another thing we noticed from the first playtest is that enemies were absurdly difficult to kill at times due to over-tuned armor values and positional bonuses from cover. We originally removed the positional bonuses, and made armor values a threshold you need to surpass, but against high armor enemies it made hitting them or doing damage nigh impossible. So we changed that back to it being a dice roll using the enemy’s defense stat.

In an earlier hotfix we changed enemy armor to be much lower, in the range of armor 1 and 2, to lower the thresholds needed to cause damage and wounds to an enemy. So far, for chaff, it’s worked quite well! But we have the other issue now of cover not helping them at all when it comes to their defense. So now that they’re more squishy, we’re re-enabling cover adjustments and possibly distance modifiers for the next session.

-Movement

Yeah we’re still trying to sort this one our ourselves too… But, we do want to emphasize player movement a bit more during combat. One thing we’re planning to push this patch is a change to cover mechanics to introduce flanking to completely negate the effects of a cover bonus. In addition, movement from cover to cover will take one turn, and you’ll be able to shoot while moving a bit easier to compensate, but also vulnerable until your next turn.

-Final Notes

I would write something witty or sappy here… But I don’t think there’s much to say that I haven’t already. All the changes will be put into the full patch notes below. We do have some future stuff planned, like a progression system for gadgets, support for 8 player co-op, changes to the gadget selection and loadout, close air support being a bit more prevalent, and then possibly improvements to vehicular combat (right now we’re basically treating it as impenetrable to small arms and extremely vulnerable to AT if its armored), and more.

But I digress. Patch notes below, send your feedback in the ⁠calamari-discussion channel if you’ve got notes and comments, and feel free to throw suggestions our way.

I could really go for some legally distinct orange soda...

-Spiny, Lead Developer and Designer
